Abstract

This portfolio presents three interconnected research projects in prediction market systems. Project 1 investigates automated market maker design and liquidity provision under varying conditions. Project 2 develops a systematic framework for identifying and trading persistent mispricings in event contracts. Project 3 delivers fault-tolerant data capture infrastructure supporting the empirical work in Projects 1 and 2.

1Market Design & Liquidity

1.1 Problem Statement

Prediction markets promise efficient aggregation of dispersed beliefs into prices, but their microstructure remains poorly understood compared to traditional financial markets. We investigate how automated market maker design choices affect price discovery quality in binary event markets.

Definition 1.1 — Adaptive-Spread LMSR

A logarithmic market scoring rule where the spread parameter b is a function of realized volatility σ and net order flow imbalance φ, such that b(t) = b₀ · g(σ, φ) where g is a monotone increasing function of both arguments.

1.2 Key Results

Empirical analysis of Polymarket and Kalshi orderbooks reveals that cost-function market makers exhibit superior price stability in thin markets but incur higher inventory costs during rapid information arrival events.

Result 1.1

Adaptive-spread LMSR reduces adverse selection costs by 34% compared to fixed-spread parameterization, measured across 1,847 event contracts with final resolution data.

2Haven Selection & Shadow Trading

2.1 Framework

Haven selection identifies prediction market contracts where prices persistently deviate from fair value due to structural factors: low liquidity, retail sentiment bias, or hedging demand from correlated positions.

Definition 2.1 — Haven Contract

A contract C is classified as a haven if it satisfies all four selection criteria simultaneously: model deviation > 8%, notional depth > $5K, mean-reversion half-life < 72h, and portfolio beta < 0.3.

2.3 Execution

Selected contracts enter a shadow-trading book where positions are sized using a modified Kelly criterion. The system is implemented as a Python async pipeline with PostgreSQL state management and exchange-specific API adapters.

3Reliable Market Data Loggers

3.1 Architecture

The empirical work in Projects 1 and 2 requires continuous, gap-free data capture across exchanges with heterogeneous API paradigms. This project delivers fault-tolerant logging infrastructure with checkpoint-recovery semantics.

Operational Result

Current deployment achieves 99.94% uptime across three exchanges (Polymarket, Kalshi, Metaculus) over 180 days of continuous operation, with ~2.1 GB/month storage in schema-versioned Parquet with Zstandard compression.
