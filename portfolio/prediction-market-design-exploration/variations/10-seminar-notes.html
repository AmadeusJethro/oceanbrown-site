Research Seminar — Prediction Markets

Systems, Microstructure, and Execution in Event Markets

Three projects · 2024–2026 · Portfolio overview

1. Market Design & Liquidity

1.1 Problem

Prediction markets aggregate dispersed beliefs into prices, but their microstructure is poorly understood. We investigate how automated market maker design choices—cost function parameterization, fee structures, liquidity provision—affect price discovery quality in binary event markets.

1.2 Approach

Comparative analysis of logarithmic market scoring rules (LMSR) vs continuous limit order books (CLOB) using synchronized orderbook data from Polymarket and Kalshi. Simulation experiments calibrated to observed parameters, then subject to synthetic information shocks.

Key Result

Adaptive-spread LMSR reduces adverse selection costs by 34% compared to fixed-spread parameterization, measured across 1,847 resolved event contracts.

The proposed liquidity-sensitive fee structure adapts bid-ask spreads dynamically based on realized volatility and order flow imbalance, drawing on the market making under inventory constraints literature.

2. Haven Selection & Shadow Trading

2.1 Framework

Haven selection identifies contracts where prices persistently deviate from fair value due to structural factors: low liquidity, retail sentiment bias, or hedging demand. The shadow-trading system executes using a relative-value framework.

3. Reliable Market Data Loggers

3.1 Architecture

Fault-tolerant logging infrastructure for continuous multi-exchange data capture. Checkpoint-recovery pattern: write-ahead logs, Parquet persistence with configurable partitioning, and supervisor-managed heartbeats for restart and backfill.

Operational Metrics

3 exchanges (Polymarket, Kalshi, Metaculus) · 99.94% uptime over 180 days · ~2.1 GB/month (Parquet + Zstd) · Schema-versioned for long-term integrity
