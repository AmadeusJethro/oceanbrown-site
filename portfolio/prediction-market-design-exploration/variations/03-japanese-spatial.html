Project I

Market Design & Liquidity in Event Markets

This project investigates the microstructure of prediction markets, comparing logarithmic market scoring rules against continuous limit order books under varying liquidity conditions.

The central question: how should a market operator parameterize automated market makers to minimize adverse selection while maintaining sufficient liquidity for price discovery in binary event contracts?

Empirical analysis of live orderbooks reveals that cost-function market makers exhibit superior price stability in thin markets. A liquidity-sensitive fee structure is proposed that adapts spreads based on realized volatility and order flow imbalance.

Project II

Haven Selection & Shadow Trading

Haven selection is a framework for identifying prediction market contracts where prices persistently deviate from fair value due to structural factors: low liquidity, retail sentiment bias, or hedging demand.

The selection algorithm scores contracts across four dimensions—pricing efficiency, liquidity depth, mean-reversion tendency, and portfolio correlation—then executes using a modified Kelly criterion constrained by binary payoff structure.

Project III

Reliable Market Data Loggers

Fault-tolerant logging infrastructure for continuous prediction market data capture across multiple exchanges with automatic gap detection, backfill, and schema versioning for long-term data integrity.

The checkpoint-recovery architecture uses write-ahead logs, Parquet persistence, and supervisor-managed heartbeats. On failure, automatic restart and REST-based backfill ensure gap-free data continuity.
