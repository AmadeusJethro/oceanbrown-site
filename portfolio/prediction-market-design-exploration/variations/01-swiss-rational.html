Market Design & Liquidity in Event Markets

Automated market maker design, cost functions, and fee structures

This project investigates the microstructure of prediction markets, comparing logarithmic market scoring rules (LMSR) against continuous limit order books (CLOB) under varying liquidity conditions. The central question: how should a market operator parameterize automated market makers to minimize adverse selection while maintaining sufficient liquidity for price discovery?

Empirical analysis of Polymarket and Kalshi orderbooks reveals that cost-function market makers exhibit superior price stability in thin markets but incur higher inventory costs during rapid information arrival. A liquidity-sensitive fee structure is proposed that adapts spreads dynamically based on realized volatility and order flow imbalance.

Methods
Simulation, empirical orderbook analysis, theoretical cost-function design
Data Sources
Polymarket WebSocket feeds, Kalshi REST API, PredictIt historical
Key Result
Adaptive-spread LMSR reduces adverse selection costs by 34% vs fixed-spread

02

Haven Selection & Shadow-Trading System

Systematic identification of persistent mispricings across prediction markets

Haven selection is a framework for identifying prediction market contracts where prices persistently deviate from fair value due to structural factors: low liquidity, retail sentiment bias, or hedging demand from correlated positions. The shadow-trading system executes against these mispricings using a relative-value approach.

The selection algorithm scores contracts across four dimensions: pricing efficiency (deviation from model-implied probability), liquidity depth, mean-reversion tendency, and correlation to existing portfolio exposures. Selected contracts enter a shadow-trading book where positions are sized using a modified Kelly criterion constrained by binary payoff structure.

Reliable Market Data Loggers

Fault-tolerant infrastructure for continuous prediction market data capture

Prediction market research requires continuous, gap-free data capture across multiple exchanges with different API paradigms. This project builds fault-tolerant logging infrastructure capable of sustained multi-exchange capture with automatic gap detection, backfill, and schema versioning.

The system uses a checkpoint-recovery architecture: each logger process maintains a write-ahead log of received messages, persists to Parquet with configurable partitioning, and registers heartbeats with a supervisor process. On failure, the supervisor restarts the logger from the last checkpoint and initiates backfill from REST endpoints to close any gaps.
