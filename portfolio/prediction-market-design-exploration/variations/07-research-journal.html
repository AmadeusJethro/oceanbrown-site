Systems, Microstructure, and Execution Research in Event Markets

Prediction Market Research Portfolio — Three Projects

Abstract

Background. Prediction markets promise efficient belief aggregation but their microstructure, execution, and data infrastructure remain under-studied relative to traditional financial markets.
Methods. Three projects: (I) comparative analysis of LMSR vs CLOB market structures using simulation and empirical orderbook data; (II) systematic haven selection and shadow-trading framework; (III) fault-tolerant multi-exchange data logging infrastructure.
Results. Adaptive-spread LMSR reduces adverse selection by 34%. Haven selection system is operational. Data loggers achieve 99.94% uptime across three exchanges over 180 days.

I. Market Design & Liquidity

This project compares logarithmic market scoring rules against continuous limit order books under varying liquidity conditions in prediction markets. The central research question: how should automated market makers be parameterized to minimize adverse selection while maintaining sufficient liquidity for price discovery in binary event contracts?

Empirical analysis of synchronized orderbook data from Polymarket and Kalshi reveals that cost-function market makers exhibit superior price stability in thin markets but incur substantially higher inventory costs during rapid information arrival.

A liquidity-sensitive fee structure adapts bid-ask spreads dynamically based on realized volatility and order flow imbalance, achieving a 34% reduction in adverse selection relative to fixed-spread parameterization.

II. Haven Selection

Haven selection identifies prediction market contracts where prices persistently deviate from fair value. The shadow-trading system executes against these mispricings using a relative-value framework with modified Kelly sizing.

The system is implemented as a Python async pipeline with PostgreSQL state management, Redis caching, and exchange-specific API adapters for execution.

III. Data Loggers

Fault-tolerant logging infrastructure captures continuous prediction market data across Polymarket, Kalshi, and Metaculus. Checkpoint-recovery architecture ensures gap-free data continuity with 99.94% uptime over 180 days.

Each logger maintains a write-ahead log, persists to Parquet with Zstandard compression (~2.1 GB/month), and reports heartbeats to a supervisor process that manages restart and REST-based backfill on failure.
