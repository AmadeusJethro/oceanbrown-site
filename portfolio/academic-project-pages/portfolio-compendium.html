Summary 01

False Edge in Prediction Markets

Most prediction-market edge claims collapsed under leakage review, venue portability testing, and execution-realism analysis. What survived was methodology, not alpha.

Why it matters

This project is best framed as a case study in falsification discipline. It shows why apparently strong quantitative stories matter less than the process used to invalidate weak ones and preserve only what survives review.

Highlights

  • Internal leakage audit invalidated the primary Polymarket backtest and turned the project from performance claims to failure analysis.
  • Portability testing across 107,427 Kalshi markets and 135 configurations found no positive edge.
  • The surviving contribution was methodology: fee-aware selection logic, explicit no-trading scope, and documented defects in Project Haven.
  • Cross-venue matching work showed that provenance accuracy and verification cost matter before any venue comparison can be trusted.

Portfolio angle

Useful for quant and energy employers because it presents audit discipline, portability testing, and infrastructure design rather than hype or trading bravado.

Summary 02

Project Haven

A fee-, slippage-, and correlation-aware system for monitoring same-day markets and ranking only the opportunities that clear explicit economic and operational thresholds.

Highlights

  • 24-hour monitoring window with a final 4-hour action window.
  • Transparent EV, break-even, fee, and slippage logic.
  • Category and underlying caps to control hidden concentration.
  • Operator-ready output: ranked candidates, exclusions, and audit artifacts.

Portfolio angle

This is the strongest productized quant-systems page because it turns a trading hypothesis into a disciplined decision engine.

Summary 03

Designing Reliable Data Loggers

A systems writeup on the progression from single-venue collectors to a unified market observatory with health, batching, and cost-aware deployment.

Highlights

  • Separated discovery, polling, trade collection, buffering, and health checks.
  • Cloud Run + Cloud SQL used as deliberately simple early production infrastructure.
  • Structured logging and stale-feed detection treated as product features.
  • Security posture extended even to public-data collection.

Portfolio angle

Strong software-engineering story for jobs that involve ETL, APIs, telemetry, and operational reliability.

Summary 05

What the PJM-MISO Seam Teaches

Formal validation showed Sharpe 0.015, DSR 0.125, PBO 0.600. The edge story collapsed. What remained is a methodology and market-structure case study.

Why it matters

This project demonstrates what happens when you apply genuinely strict validation to a backtested spread strategy. The discoveries of a lookahead bug and a measurement error preceding final validation make it a concrete case study in the gap between bias-free and statistically robust.

Highlights

  • A temporal alignment bug in the partial-hour feature pipeline was discovered by external review, not internal audit. 59% of apparent predictive power came from leaked data.
  • Switching from hub-based to interface-specific pricing invalidated all early results and restructured the model entirely.
  • Formal CSCV/DSR/PBO validation returned Sharpe 0.015, DSR 0.125, PBO 0.600: all failing standard thresholds.
  • Feature sprawl failed consistently across 2,500+ configurations: simple features beat every complex alternative.

Portfolio angle

Strong for energy and quant roles because it demonstrates rigorous validation discipline, real failure analysis, and market-structure intuition rather than claims of profitable edge.

Summary 04

Evaluating Agentic Systems for Quant Workflows

A benchmark and orchestration project focused on making specialized agent systems safer, more auditable, and more useful for quantitative research.

Highlights

  • Artifact-first evaluation rather than text-first scoring.
  • Role-specialized routing with compliance and review checkpoints.
  • Release gates for human quality evidence, reproducibility, holdout use, and routing proof.
  • Grounded-case benchmarking for failure modes common in time-series and backtesting work.

Portfolio angle

A strong way to present your agentic systems work as research infrastructure rather than generic AI tooling.
